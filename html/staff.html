Dr. Elena Marrowfield

Archaeologist — Artifact Analysis

Dr. Elena Marrowfield earned her PhD in Archaeology from the University of Chicago and has spent over fifteen years excavating sites across Central America and the Mediterranean. She specializes in stratigraphic excavation and artifact cataloging, with a focus on pottery, tools, and ancient structures. In the field, she employs advanced technologies such as LIDAR mapping, ground-penetrating radar, and digital field logging to document discoveries with precision. Elena is meticulous and patient, approaching every dig with a balance of scientific rigor and curiosity. She trains and supervises excavation teams, ensuring that artifacts are carefully preserved and cataloged. Beyond her research, she contributes to the museum’s exhibits by interpreting findings for public display and educational programs. She has received a national preservation grant and is recognized for her advocacy in ethical archaeology and heritage protection.

Museum Role: Leads artifact research and oversees excavation teams contributing to museum collections.

Special Considerations: Recipient of a national preservation grant and advocate for ethical archaeology.

Dr. Isaac Redfern

Physical Anthropologist — Human Remains

Dr. Isaac Redfern holds degrees in Anthropology and Biology, with a focus on human osteology and bioarchaeology. He has over a decade of experience studying human remains from archaeological contexts, examining skeletal evidence to reconstruct past diets, health patterns, and cultural practices. His expertise includes isotopic analysis, forensic measurement, and comparative skeletal studies. Dr. Redfern approaches his work with sensitivity and respect, recognizing the cultural and ethical responsibilities involved in handling human remains. Within the museum, he researches human bones and advises curators on ethical documentation, storage, and display. He develops innovative visualization methods to communicate findings to the public and collaborates with descendant communities to ensure culturally appropriate interpretation. Isaac’s careful, methodical style is complemented by his dedication to mentoring young researchers and promoting ethical standards in archaeology.

Museum Role: Researches human remains and advises on ethical documentation and display practices.

Special Considerations: Develops alternative visualization methods and collaborates with descendant communities.

Mira Solenne

Cultural Historian & Symbolic Analyst

Mira Solenne is a cultural historian specializing in symbolic analysis and post-colonial theory. She holds advanced degrees in Cultural History and Semiotics and has extensive experience interpreting artifacts, oral histories, and cultural narratives from diverse societies. Mira works closely with artists, educators, and curators to create immersive museum experiences that translate complex historical and cultural information into engaging, accessible exhibits. Her interpretive methods include cross-referencing artifacts with myths, cultural memory, and AI-assisted modeling to reconstruct the social and spiritual lives of past peoples. Mira is a passionate advocate for repatriation and challenges conventional museum narratives, highlighting marginalized voices and underrepresented histories. Her work bridges the gap between evidence and understanding, transforming physical artifacts into compelling cultural stories that resonate with the public. She is collaborative, innovative, and deeply committed to making museums spaces for inclusive learning and meaningful engagement.

Interpretive Method: Cross-references artifacts with myths, cultural memory, and AI-assisted modeling.

Special Considerations: Advocates for repatriation and challenges traditional museum narratives.
